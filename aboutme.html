About Me

I am a postdoctoral scholar in the BSIM Group at the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences, University of California, Berkeley, under the guidance of Prof. Sayeef Salahuddin and Prof. Chenming Hu.

Before that, I completed my Ph.D. in the Device Research Lab (DiRac Lab), Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, Uttarakhand, India. I was working as a Prime Minister's Research Fellow (PMRF) under the joint supervision of Prof. Avirup Dasgupta, Prof. Anand Bulusu, and Dr. Shruti Mehrotra (GlobalFoundries, India).

I received my B.E. degree from Sant Longowal Institute of Engineering and Technology (SLIET), India, in 2020. In B.E., I was awarded the silver medal for academic excellence (2020) and a tuition fee waiver for an All India Rank (AIR)-6 in the SLIET entrance test (SET-III) 2017. I am an AIR-568 holder in the GATE 2020 exam. Besides, I also received the Institute Gold Medal during my Polytechnic Diploma in 2017.

My research focuses on developing compact models for future-generation transistors and emerging memory technologies, including ferroelectric and ULTRARAM devices. Additionally, I am involved in TCAD simulations for reliability studies, including low-frequency noise, thermal noise, and NBTI.

Outside of academia, I enjoy swimming, cycling, and trekking, among other activities. I am also actively involved in volunteering at social events, demonstrating my commitment to making a positive impact beyond the academic realm.

My hometown is Bareilly, a city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, known for its rich Zardozi embroidery, thriving bamboo and furniture craftsmanship, and its historical significance, particularly its role in India’s 1857 freedom movement (First War of Independence).
