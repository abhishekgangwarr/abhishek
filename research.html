Ferroelectric Devices

Ferroelectric devices based on doped hafnium oxide (HfO₂) are promising for low-power logic, nonvolatile memory (NVM), and in-memory computing due to CMOS compatibility and low-power operation. NCFET can overcome the 60 mV/dec subthreshold limit and provide internal voltage amplification, higher ON current, and lower threshold voltage.

We study device physics, design, and compact modeling of Si-FeFET and FE-HEMT for storage and NC-CFET for logic applications.

ULTRARAM

ULTRARAM is an emerging non-classical charge-trapping NVM offering fast operation, high endurance, long retention, and ultra-low switching energy. Its novelty lies in InAs/AlSb triple-barrier resonant tunneling (TBRT), enabling fast program/erase with low energy.

We developed the first physics-based compact model of ULTRARAM capturing charge trapping/detrapping in floating-gate structures and applied it to circuit-level design and neuromorphic use-cases.

ML for EDA

Machine learning is transforming EDA by improving efficiency, scalability, and predictive capability. We work on ML-assisted semiconductor device optimization and neural-network-augmented compact modeling frameworks for advanced technology nodes.
